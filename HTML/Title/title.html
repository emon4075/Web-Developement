HTML Page Title Guide

The current page title is: "HTML Page Title Examples - Learn HTML Titles"

What is a Page Title?

The HTML page title is defined by the <title> element in the document head. It appears in:

  • Browser tabs and title bars
  • Search engine results (SERP)
  • Social media shares
  • Bookmarks and browser history
  • When pages are shared or saved

Title Tag Syntax

<title>Your Page Title Here</title>

Good Title Examples

E-commerce Product Page:

<title>iPhone 15 Pro - 128GB Space Black | Apple Store</title>

Why it's good: Specific product, key details, brand name

Blog Article:

<title>10 Best CSS Grid Techniques for 2025 | Web Dev Blog</title>

Why it's good: Descriptive, includes year for freshness, site name

Company About Page:

<title>About TechCorp - Leading Software Solutions Since 2010</title>

Why it's good: Clear purpose, company positioning, established date

Local Business:

<title>Mario's Pizza - Best Italian Restaurant in New York | Order Online</title>

Why it's good: Business name, location, key selling point, call-to-action

Poor Title Examples

Too Generic:

<title>Home Page</title>

Why it's bad: Doesn't describe content or purpose

Too Long:

<title>Welcome to our amazing website where you can find everything you need for your home, garden, pets, electronics, clothing, books, music, movies and much more at the best prices with fast shipping</title>

Why it's bad: Too long, gets cut off in search results

Keyword Stuffing:

<title>Pizza, Best Pizza, NYC Pizza, Italian Pizza, Cheap Pizza, Pizza Delivery</title>

Why it's bad: Spammy, poor user experience, may be penalized

Missing Context:

<title>Page 2</title>

Why it's bad: No indication of content or site

Title Best Practices

Length Guidelines

  • Optimal: 50-60 characters
  • Maximum display: ~70 characters in Google
  • Consider: Different search engines have different limits

Structure Patterns

Primary Keyword | Secondary Keyword | Brand

<title>Running Shoes | Athletic Footwear | SportStore</title>

Page Name - Site Name

<title>Contact Us - TechCorp Solutions</title>

Descriptive Title: Subtitle | Brand

<title>Web Development: Complete Beginner's Guide | CodeAcademy</title>

Dynamic Titles with JavaScript

You can change page titles dynamically:

document.title = "New Page Title";

Title for Different Page Types

Homepage

<title>CompanyName - Brief Description of What You Do</title>

Category Pages

<title>Men's Clothing | Fashion Store</title>

Product Pages

<title>Product Name - Key Feature | Store Name</title>

Blog Posts

<title>Article Title | Blog Name</title>

Error Pages

<title>404 - Page Not Found | Site Name</title>

SEO Considerations

  • Unique titles: Every page should have a unique title
  • Relevant keywords: Include primary keywords naturally
  • Brand consistency: Include brand name consistently
  • User intent: Match what users are searching for
  • Compelling copy: Make users want to click

Testing Your Titles

Tools to preview how your titles appear:

  • Google Search Console
  • SEO browser extensions
  • Social media sharing debuggers
  • SERP preview tools
